Sure, I can definitely help you with that! There are 12 tenses in English, which are divided into four categories: simple, progressive, perfect, and perfect progressive. Here are the 12 tenses in English with examples:

Simple present tense: Used to describe actions that are currently happening or actions that happen regularly.
Example: I drink coffee every morning.

Simple past tense: Used to describe actions that happened in the past.
Example: She walked to the store yesterday.

Simple future tense: Used to describe actions that will happen in the future.
Example: They will arrive at the airport tomorrow.

Present progressive tense: Used to describe actions that are currently happening in the present.
Example: I am writing an email right now.

Past progressive tense: Used to describe actions that were happening in the past.
Example: She was studying for her exam last night.

Future progressive tense: Used to describe actions that will be happening at a specific point in the future.
Example: They will be flying to Paris next week.

Present perfect tense: Used to describe actions that started in the past and continue in the present.
Example: I have lived in this city for five years.

Past perfect tense: Used to describe actions that were completed before another action in the past.
Example: By the time she arrived, he had already left.

Future perfect tense: Used to describe actions that will be completed before a specific point in the future.
Example: By this time next year, they will have been married for 10 years.

Present perfect progressive tense: Used to describe actions that started in the past and continue up to the present.
Example: I have been studying English for three years.

Past perfect progressive tense: Used to describe actions that were happening over a period of time before another action in the past.
Example: She had been working for the company for 10 years when she was promoted.

Future perfect progressive tense: Used to describe actions that will continue up to a specific point in the future.
Example: By next year, I will have been living in this city for eight years.
